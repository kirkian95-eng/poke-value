How EV is Calculated

Expected Value Formula

The Expected Value (EV) of a booster pack represents the average dollar value you'd receive if you opened many packs. It's calculated by multiplying each card's market price by its probability of being pulled, then summing across all cards in the set.

EV = Σ P(card) × Price(card) for all cards in the set.

Pack Slot Model

Modern Pokemon TCG packs (Scarlet & Violet era) contain 10 game cards with three slot types:

Slot TypeCountHow It Works
Guaranteed Common 4 4 random commons from the set's common pool. P(specific card) = 4 / Ncommon
Guaranteed Uncommon 3 3 random uncommons from the set's uncommon pool. P(specific card) = 3 / Nuncommon
Reverse Holo 2 2 random reverse holo cards from the common+uncommon+rare pool. Priced at ~50% of normal value.
Hit Slot (Rare+) 1 One card that can range from base Rare all the way up to Hyper Rare, based on pull rate probabilities.

Hit Slot Probability

The "hit slot" is where the value comes from. It has a probability distribution across rarities. These probabilities come from community-compiled data (PokeBeach, Reddit, YouTube openings) since The Pokemon Company does not officially publish pull rates.

P(specific card) = P(rarity) / Ncards of that rarity

Default rates are based on Scarlet & Violet era averages and can be overridden per set for more accurate calculations.

God Pack Adjustment

Some sets include "God Packs" where every card in the pack is a rare or higher. When a set has god packs, the EV is adjusted:

EV = (1 - Pgod) × Normal EV + Pgod × God Pack EV

God pack odds are typically 1 in 600-1000 packs depending on the set.

Disclaimers

  • Pull rates are estimates based on community data, not official figures.
  • Prices update periodically, not in real-time.
  • EV represents mathematical expectation — individual pack results vary widely.
  • Most sealed Pokemon products have negative EV (you pay more than the cards are worth on average).
  • Reverse holo pricing uses a 50% approximation of normal card price.
